BCIT GIS Practicum - Andrew Dollak

Eagle Mapping 2025

⭐ Aerial Imagery Collection and Processing ⭐

Abstract

This practicum with Eagle Mapping focused on aerial imagery collection and processing techniques for topographic mapping and GIS analysis. Working with industry-standard equipment and software, I participated in flight planning, image acquisition, and data processing workflows to produce high-quality orthoimagery and digital elevation models. The project addressed challenges in data management, quality control, and integration with existing GIS systems while adhering to industry specifications for spatial accuracy.

Eagle Mapping Project Execution:

The Eagle Mapping project covered the Germansen Landing and Manson Creek area in British Columbia. The survey mission was carefully planned to ensure complete coverage and optimal data quality.

Mission Parameters:
  • Coverage area: 15 square kilometers
  • 12 flight lines at 9300-9800 ft altitude
  • 60% forward overlap, 30% side overlap
  • Camera: PhaseOne iXM 100MP
  • Ground sampling distance: 10cm
  • Products: orthoimagery and digital elevation model
Quality Control:

Flight lines were carefully designed to account for terrain variation, ensuring consistent image quality and complete coverage of the target area. Ground control points were established to achieve horizontal accuracy of ±15cm and vertical accuracy of ±25-30cm in the final products.

Data Acquisition
Cessna 210 with RIEGL 780IIS LiDAR scanner
Equipment Used:
  • Aircraft: Cessna 210
  • Camera: PhaseOne iXM 100MP
  • LiDAR Scanner: RIEGL 780IIS
  • GNSS/IMU positioning system
  • Flight control system with automated navigation
Flight Parameters:
  • Flying height: 1,200m AGL
  • Ground sampling distance: 10cm
  • Image overlap: 60% forward, 30% side
  • LiDAR point density: 8 points/m²
